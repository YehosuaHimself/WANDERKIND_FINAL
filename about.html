Every Wanderkind is a Host

Wanderkind is not a service. It is a road, and the road has houses. Every traveler is also someone's host, and every host was once a traveler. That symmetry is the whole thing.

The three duties

When you keep a door open on Wanderkind, you commit to three small acts. Not all at once, and not for every walker — only what you can do.

⌂
Open the door
A roof, a bed, a corner of the garden — whatever you can offer. The walker steps in. You step back.
◓
Share the table
Bread, soup, what's already on the stove. Hosting is not a restaurant. It is an extra plate.
◊
Pass the water
The smallest gift, the oldest one. A bottle filled, a fountain pointed to. Nobody walks dry.

No money. No score.

There is no payment between walker and host. There is no rating. Money would turn the road into a market, and a number would turn the door into a transaction. Instead there is the morning vouch — two short lines, written blind, revealed together — and a stamp in the Wanderbuch. That is the only record.

"The walker is not a guest. The host is not a service. They are two Wanderkinder who met on the road."

What this app is

A pass you can show. A map you can read. A door you can open. Some hashes that let strangers trust each other without surrendering their privacy. Everything else — the conversation, the bread, the morning — is between you and the road.
